Coaching center for Class 8–12 students. Run by one teacher with two part-time tutors. 60 students enrolled. Exam prep focused. No admin support.

Nagpur
What he's paid to do

Admission posts
3 pain points
1
Writing test papers every week
Every Sunday he creates a fresh question paper from scratch — pulling questions from memory and old textbooks. It takes 2–3 hours with no system and no templates. By Monday he's already exhausted.
"I spend more time making the test than reviewing the results."
2
Parent communication
Progress updates, fee reminders, batch change notices, exam schedules — all typed manually on WhatsApp. Repetitive and often delayed because it feels like extra work on top of teaching.
"Parents complain they don't hear from me. But when do I have time to write?"

Admission season content
New batch starting? He needs social media posts, a WhatsApp broadcast, a notice board poster. He either skips it or posts something plain that gets no response — and loses students to centers with better-looking content.
"I know I should promote more. I just don't know how to write these things."

Little Blooms Preschool

A new preschool started by a first-time owner. Catering to 2–5 year olds. Trying to fill 30 seats before opening day. No marketing background, no admin support.

Pune
What she's paid to do
Care for young children
Build a safe space
Earn parent trust
What overwhelms her mornings
Promoting admissions
Answering same questions
Parent newsletters
3 pain points
1
Promoting admissions
She needs parents in her neighborhood to know the school exists. But writing Facebook posts and WhatsApp forwards feels hard — she doesn't know what to say or how to sound trustworthy. Her posts get no response.
"I know my school is good. I just can't explain it in words that make people call me."
2
Answering the same parent questions
Every inquiry call covers the same ground — timings, fees, what children learn, is food provided, teacher-child ratio. She's answering this 10 times a day with no FAQ document and no saved message to share.
"I repeat myself on every call. By the 8th parent I'm exhausted before the day starts."
3
Monthly parent newsletters
Enrolled parents want to feel connected — what did kids learn this week, what's coming next month, any events. She knows this builds loyalty and reduces dropouts. But writing it consistently never happens.
"I take photos all day. But turning them into something to send parents — I never get around to it."

Your opportunity as a student
She's brilliant with children. The communication around the school is what's slipping. You can build her a simple content system she just maintains — while you produce all the first drafts.

Priya's Home Bakery

A home baker selling custom cakes via Instagram and WhatsApp. Solo operation. Orders taken manually. Growing faster than she can manage alone.

Mumbai
What she's paid to do
Bake and decorate
Deliver on time
Create custom designs
What pulls her from the kitchen
Instagram every day
WhatsApp back-and-forth
Missing seasonal sales
3 pain points
1
Instagram content every day
She bakes beautiful things but posting them with the right caption, hashtags, and timing takes time she doesn't have. She either posts late, posts plainly, or skips days — which hurts her reach and order flow.
"I spend an hour baking something and five minutes posting it badly."
2
WhatsApp order enquiries
Every order is a long back-and-forth — flavor, size, date, budget. She's typing the same questions to every customer. No standard order form, no saved replies. Conversations get lost. Orders get confused.
"I got two orders mixed up last month. The customers were upset. I was mortified."
3
Missing seasonal sales windows
Diwali boxes, Valentine's hampers, Christmas cakes — her biggest revenue moments. But planning the offers, writing the posts, creating the menu all happens last minute. She misses the window every time.
"By the time I post my Diwali offer, everyone has already ordered from someone else."
💡
Your opportunity as a student
Priya's product sells itself — her cakes are beautiful. What she's missing is a content system and a sales rhythm. You can build both. And because your work directly connects to her sales, she'll feel the value immediately.

Before — WhatsApp chaos
Every order is a long back-and-forth. Flavor? Size? Date? Message on cake? She types the same questions to every customer. Orders get mixed up. Revenue is lost.

Sharma Classes
Replacing phone-tag with a Calendly booking link for parent visits
Demo 2
Before — phone chaos
Parents call during class to schedule visits. Sharma sir misses calls, double-books, forgets appointments. Some parents give up and choose another center.
After — Calendly link
Parent clicks a link, picks a slot, gets an automatic confirmation. Sharma sir sees all appointments in his calendar. No calls. No double bookings. No no-shows.
Step 1 — AI writes the booking page
Live prompt — run in class
"Write a short description for a Calendly booking page for Sharma Classes, a coaching center in Nagpur for Class 8–12 students. Parents can book a 20-minute visit slot to see the center and meet the teacher. Keep it warm, professional, and under 60 words. Also write the confirmation email the parent receives after booking."
Step 2 — Set up Calendly
1
Create a free Calendly account
Sign up free at calendly.com using Google. The free plan allows one event type — all Sharma sir needs.
calendly.com → Sign up free
2
Create the event type
New Event Type → One-on-one → 20 minutes. Name it "Parent Visit — Sharma Classes". Paste the AI-written description.
3
Set available hours
e.g. Mon, Wed, Fri 5–7 PM only. Parents can only book within these windows. Teaching hours are automatically blocked.
Availability → Set weekly hours
4
Paste the AI confirmation email
Include the address, what to bring, and what to expect. This sends automatically to every parent who books.
Notifications → Confirmation email → Edit
5
Share via WhatsApp quick reply
Set up a /visit quick reply in WhatsApp Business with the Calendly link. Any parent who asks to visit gets the link in one tap.
WhatsApp Business → Quick Replies → /visit
